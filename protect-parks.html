Protect National Parks

Everyday visitor choices help keep parks wild, safe, and beautiful for future generations.

Why Protection Matters

National parks protect fragile ecosystems, clean water, wildlife habitat, and cultural resources. Heavy visitation can cause trail erosion, wildlife stress, litter, water pollution, and damage to historic sites if visitors are not careful.

Small actions, such as staying on trail or packing out trash, make a measurable difference when multiplied by millions of yearly visitors.

Leave No Trace: 7 Principles

  1. Plan ahead and prepare: Know rules, weather, closures, and permits before you arrive.
  2. Travel and camp on durable surfaces: Stay on marked trails and established campsites.
  3. Dispose of waste properly: Pack out all trash, leftover food, and litter.
  4. Leave what you find: Do not move rocks, pick flowers, or remove cultural or natural objects.
  5. Minimize campfire impacts: Follow fire restrictions and use established fire rings when allowed.
  6. Respect wildlife: Keep safe distance and never feed animals.
  7. Be considerate of others: Keep noise low, yield on trails, and protect everyone’s outdoor experience.

Practical Things You Can Do Today

  • Bring a trash bag and leave with more than you brought in.
  • Use refillable water bottles instead of single-use plastics.
  • Stay on designated trails even when shortcuts look tempting.
  • Keep food secured so wildlife does not become food-conditioned.
  • Use restroom facilities; if unavailable, follow local waste rules.
  • Respect closures that protect restoration zones and nesting areas.
  • Choose shuttle or carpool options to reduce traffic and emissions.

Protect Wildlife and Habitat

Wildlife behavior changes when people get too close or feed animals. Keeping distance protects both visitors and animals. Plants and soils can also take years to recover from trampling.

  • Observe with binoculars or zoom instead of approaching.
  • Never leave food scraps, even organic ones like fruit peels.
  • Do not stack rocks or build structures in natural areas.
  • Keep pets where allowed and always follow leash rules.

Protect Cultural and Historic Sites

Many parks include sacred Indigenous sites, historic buildings, and archaeology that cannot be replaced once damaged.

  • Do not touch rock art, carvings, or old masonry surfaces.
  • Stay behind barriers and follow all interpretation signs.
  • Take photos only, and leave artifacts where they are found.
